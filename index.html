I've always been a dreamer. From an early age, I spent a lot of time building my own parking lots because I loved parking cars. I could repeat the same parking procedure over and over again, and somehow I could always find a way to make the parking lots more entertaining.

I began to realize what reality is. The girl that once had a big dream of becoming a singer or a doctor had to encounter the fact that not anyone can become a singer or a doctor. Sometimes it's more about luck, family background, and perfect grades.

I had to find myself again. And that's why I'm here, writing this down on the website I learned from one of my courses in NCU. I know this should be an autobiography, but it would be too boring if I write it in plain white sheet with dark ink of Times New Roman Size 12.

Why and What

Why
Originally, I didn't plan to study at NCU, I was actually trying to apply for the International College of Innovation at NCCU. I even had all the paper ready. However, someone told me that I could apply for the 繁星計畫, which I did and successfully got accepted to the Department of English at NCU. But as four semesters have gone by, I started to realize that the things that I want to learn aren't here. There's no denying that I learned a lot of critical thinking from the courses I've chosen, but these courses just don't click with me. I wanted something more realistic. Something that could actually be put into use.
What
